Teaching

Thomas leads the Applied Health Economics module on Lancaster's online Health Economics and Policy programme, covering payment systems, incentives in health care, insurance, moral hazard and adverse selection; he previously led the programme's Data Analysis module, covering regression, panel data techniques and causal inference.

Before Lancaster, he taught the economics of health on the MSc Economics programme at Manchester (supply, regulation and financing; incentives), and econometrics to final-year BSc Economics students, for which he received a departmental teaching award with student ratings in the top 5% of tutors.

PhD supervision

Thomas supervises part-time, distance-based PhD students at Lancaster across a range of topics in health economics and policy, currently including the health outcomes and costs of AI-assisted diagnostic imaging in breast cancer, and completed doctoral supervision at Manchester on the economic impact of psoriasis.

Prospective PhD students

Thomas welcomes enquiries from prospective PhD students, including those studying part-time and at a distance, in: applied econometrics and policy evaluation in health; the health and care workforce; the economics of addiction; the economics of AI adoption in health care; the financing and organisation of care; and inequalities in health. Many of his students come to quantitative research from professional rather than academic backgrounds; that is not a barrier, and the blended-learning route at Lancaster is designed for it. Prospective applicants are welcome to email him a short outline (a page is plenty) of the question they want to answer and the data they think might answer it.
